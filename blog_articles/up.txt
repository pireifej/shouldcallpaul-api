    <p class="byline">by Sherri Rase</p>
    
    <p>Joe DiPietro is a Jersey boy. He "writes" Jersey, and is most known for writing his characters in such a natural style that the dialogue comes sui generis from their lips rather than from a writer's pen. An East Coast premiere, "The Last Romance" is a play written originally for Marion Ross and her husband Paul Michael, who became friends with DiPietro and, while they are not mirrors of Carol and Ralph, there are elements of each actor in each of these characters and is part of what makes them SO real.</p>

    <p>J.C. Hoyt is Ralph, the octogenarian, whom we first meet sitting in a dog park, clearly waiting for someone. Hoyt's Ralph is a sparky, sparkly gentleman of mature years, who comes from that Greatest Generation of Depression-era young adults, who did things because they were the right things to do. This will be an important point during the reveal of the plot twist in Act Two.
