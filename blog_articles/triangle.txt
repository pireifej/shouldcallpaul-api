    <h1>The Brothers Size - Reverberating Truths</h1>
    <p class="byline">By Sherri Rase</p>
    
    
    <p class="entry-content" style="border: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 1.625em 0px 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"></p><p style="color: rgb(55, 55, 55); font-family: inherit; font-size: 15px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">By Sherri Rase</p><p style="display: inline-block;margin: 20px;text-align: center;"><img src="https://shouldcallpaul.replit.app/img/triangle.jpg" alt="Description of image"><span style="font-style: italic;"><br></span></p><p><p style="display: inline-block;margin: 20px;text-align: center;"><span style="font-style: italic;">Back: Brandon Carter, Front: L: Clinton Lowe, R: Shamsuddin Abdul-HamidPhoto Credit: Christopher Drukker</span></p><p></p><p style="color: rgb(55, 55, 55); font-family: inherit; font-size: 15px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Tarell Alvin McCraney's play "The Brothers Size" was written back in 2007 when McCraney was in his third year as a student at Yale School of Drama.&nbsp; Though it was written nearly 10 years ago, it reverberates with that knell that resonates deep in the heart like the lowest thrumming notes of a pipe organ.&nbsp; So many overtones to that note and so deep it is almost below the human capacity to hear.&nbsp; But it is felt.</p><p style="color: rgb(55, 55, 55); font-family: inherit; font-size: 15px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Combining Yoruba mythology for whom his characters named with an all too familiar urban tale, we have two sets of brothers.&nbsp; Ogun (Brandon Carter) is the eldest brother Size who's always felt the weight of providing for and protecting his family.&nbsp; Their mother died when both boys were so very young and Oshoosi (Shamsuddin Abdul-Haid) ever since that time has always found himself on the wrong side of most situations.&nbsp; When the action of the play begins, Oshoosi has just completed serving time in prison and it's made him bone-deep exhausted and as enervated as it's possible for a human to be.&nbsp; Set in bayou country, it's easy to image humidity so deep you can swim in it and Oshoosi feels every molecule of its weight.&nbsp; Ogun does his best, but motivating Oshoosi is like herding cats.&nbsp; Oshoosi's not lazy, but his nightmares have him running full out in a way Ogun has yet to fully fathom.</p><p style="color: rgb(55, 55, 55); font-family: inherit; font-size: 15px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Oshoosi's other brother is one of circumstance, his buddy Elegba (Clinton Lowe) with whom he bonded in prison.&nbsp; There's a thrumming current between them and it's only later that we see the web that Elegba has woven.&nbsp; Elegba's sinuous machinations and smooth ways of influencing Oshoosi could be based in his own psyche or possibly his knowledge of what motivates his young friend, the dreamer.&nbsp; Elegba was Oshoosi's "brother in need" during his time in prison when Oshoosi missed Ogun like an amputee misses a limb.&nbsp; Until the end of the play, however, it's not clear fully what that means.&nbsp; Elegba has a level of influence parallel to Ogun's but very different in affect.</p><p style="color: rgb(55, 55, 55); font-family: inherit; font-size: 15px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Oshoosi's special talent is seeing and believing the best in everyone, yet this is what leads him down the primrose path time and time again.&nbsp; When Ogun makes the penultimate sacrifice for Oshoosi, there's no doubt of this brother's love.&nbsp; The question is always what will we give so others may live.</p><p style="color: rgb(55, 55, 55); font-family: inherit; font-size: 15px; border: 0px; font-style: inherit; font-weight: inherit; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Be sure to visit the Context Room, a very new feature of Luna Stage productions.&nbsp;
