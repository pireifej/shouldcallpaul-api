&nbsp;Encourage your body to “feel” something on the outside and ultimately it will penetrate within.</p><p style="border: 0px; font-family: &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 15px; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(55, 55, 55);">What is challenging you right now? &nbsp;Are you overwhelmed with work? &nbsp;Are you snowed in by family or volunteer obligations? &nbsp;First things first – step back and give yourself a little room to breathe. &nbsp;What would make you happy in terms of this challenge – what would be an appropriate resolution? &nbsp;You solve problems this way for others, don’t you deserve the same care?</p><p style="border: 0px; font-family: &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 15px; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(55, 55, 55);">John Stuart Mill was a philosopher whose theory was the best course of action is that which provides the greatest good for all, rather than the individual. &nbsp;Sometimes though, you need to take care of yourself first so that you can take care of others. &nbsp;Remember to do the things that make you feel most like yourself – eat well, shower regularly, get enough sleep – as a post-Modern adult, I fall down on some of these (I DO love my hot showers…) but when I’m on top of them, my best foot is firmly forward.</p><p style="border: 0px; font-family: &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 15px; margin-bottom: 1.625em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; color: rgb(55, 55, 55);">Keep up appearances – not for others, but for yourself. &nbsp;When you feel like YOU, you’re able to achieve more and be the best part of yourself.</p>
